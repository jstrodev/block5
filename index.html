Bears

Bears are omnivores, which means they eat both plants and animals. They need a lot of space to roam and explore, and they love to swim. Bears are also very intelligent and curious animals. They need a lot of mental stimulation to keep them happy and healthy. Ollie likes fish and Mona likes berries.

  2. Mona

Giraffes

Giraffes are herbivores, which means they eat only plants. They need a lot of fresh leaves and twigs to eat. Giraffes are also very social animals and need to be around other giraffes to be happy. Frankie likes to eat acacia leaves and Coconut likes to eat leaves from the mimosa tree.

  1. Frankie
  2. Coconut
Learn more about Giraffes

Lions

Lions are carnivores, which means they eat only meat. They need a lot of space to roam and explore, and they love to sleep. Lions are also very social animals and need to be around other lions to be happy. Mella likes to eat zebra and Karl likes to eat wildebeest.

  1. Mella
  2. Karl
Learn more about Lions

Monkeys

Monkeys are omnivores, which means they eat both plants and animals. They need a lot of fresh fruit and insects to eat. Monkeys are also very social animals and need to be around other monkeys to be happy. Cookie likes to eat bananas and Earl likes to eat mangoes. Banana Pudding likes to eat both bananas and mangoes.

  1. Cookie
  2. Earl
  3. Banana Pudding
Learn more about Monkeys

Alligators

Alligators are carnivores, which means they eat only meat. They need a lot of fresh fish and small mammals to eat. Alligators are also very territorial animals and need to be in their own space to be happy. Wren likes to eat fish and Aspen likes to eat small mammals. Mika likes to eat both fish and small mammals.

  2. Aspen
  3. Mika
